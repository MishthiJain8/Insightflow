[CEO REMARKS]
Good morning, everyone. Thank you for joining us today.

I want to begin by saying that Q4 was a defining quarter for our company.
Despite a complex macroeconomic environment marked by elevated interest rates
and global supply chain normalization, we delivered results that exceeded our
own internal guidance on three of four key metrics.

Revenue grew 18.4% year-over-year, driven by strong demand in our enterprise
segment, which now contributes 63% of total revenue. Our cloud and digital
services division crossed a significant milestone, achieving annualized
recurring revenue of $4.2 billion, ahead of schedule.

On margins: gross margin expanded 120 basis points to 71.3%. Operating margin
came in at 28.6%, reflecting disciplined cost management even as we continue to
invest aggressively in R&D and talent.

We added 3,200 net new employees this quarter, predominantly in engineering and
AI-related functions. Our headcount in AI-focused roles now stands at over
12,000 globally — a 40% increase year-on-year.

Looking at geographic performance: our India business delivered exceptional
results, with 31% revenue growth year-over-year. Asia-Pacific more broadly was
our fastest-growing region. North America grew 14%, and Europe, while impacted
by currency headwinds, held steady at 9% constant currency growth.

I want to spend a moment on our AI strategy. We believe we are entering what I
describe as the "second inflection point" in enterprise AI adoption. The first
wave was about exploration — proof-of-concepts, sandboxes, small pilots. We are
now seeing large-scale, production-grade deployments from our Tier-1 clients.

Our proprietary AI platform, internally called Aurora, processed over 2.8
trillion inference calls this quarter alone — a 220% year-on-year increase.

Sentiment is genuinely positive, and I believe we are in a strong position
heading into FY2026. Our pipeline is the healthiest it has ever been.

[CFO REMARKS]
Thank you. I will now walk through the financials in more detail.

Total revenue for Q4: $6.84 billion, up 18.4% year-over-year and 4.2%
sequentially, ahead of consensus by approximately $180 million.

Free cash flow came in at $1.9 billion, with a free cash flow margin of 27.8%.
We ended the quarter with $14.3 billion in cash and short-term investments on
the balance sheet.

Returning capital to shareholders: we repurchased $800 million in shares this
quarter and paid dividends of $220 million, for a total capital return of over
$1 billion in Q4 alone.

EPS (non-GAAP diluted): $2.14, up 22% year-over-year, beating consensus by $0.11.

[ANALYST Q&A - SELECTED EXCERPTS]

Q: Can you elaborate on the competitive dynamics in the AI infrastructure space?

CEO: Absolutely. What we are seeing is a consolidation of enterprise spend
around a small number of trusted platform providers. The "build vs. buy"
conversation is shifting decisively toward buy — clients want integrated,
auditable, compliant AI systems. We believe our Aurora platform is the most
enterprise-ready option in the market today. The tone from customers is one of
urgency, not caution. They don't want to be left behind.

Q: Regarding guidance for Q1 FY2026?

CFO: We are guiding Q1 revenue in the range of $7.1 to $7.25 billion, implying
approximately 16 to 18 percent growth year-over-year. Non-GAAP EPS guidance:
$2.20 to $2.26. We feel good about this range and believe it is appropriately
calibrated given the macro backdrop.
